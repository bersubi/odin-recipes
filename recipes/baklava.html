Baklava

Baklava is a layered pastry dessert made of filo pastry, filled with chopped nuts, and sweetened with syrup or honey. It was one of the most popular sweet pastries of Ottoman cuisine.

The pre-Ottoman origin of the dish is unknown, but, in modern times, it is a common dessert of Turkish, Iranian and Arab cuisines, and other countries of the Levant and Maghreb, along with the South Caucasus, Greece, Balkans, Somalia and Central Asia.

Ingredients

  • For the filling:

    • 1½ cups chopped nuts (walnuts, pistachios, or a combination)
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

  • For the layers:

    • 1 package (16 ounces) phyllo dough, thawed
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted

  • For the syrup:

    • 1 cup water
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • ½ cup honey
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

Steps

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a bowl, combine the chopped nuts, sugar, and ground cinnamon. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the Phyllo Dough:

    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • Trim the phyllo dough to fit the size of your baking dish if necessary.
    • Place a damp cloth over the phyllo sheets to prevent them from drying out as you work.

  3. Assemble the Layers:

    • Brush a layer of melted butter on the bottom of a baking dish (usually 9x13 inches).
    • Place a sheet of phyllo dough in the dish and brush it with melted butter. Repeat until you have about 6 layers.
    • Sprinkle a portion of the nut mixture evenly over the buttered phyllo layers.
    • Continue layering phyllo and butter until you've used about half of the phyllo sheets.
    • Add another layer of nuts.
    • Finish layering the remaining phyllo sheets on top, brushing each layer with butter.

  4. Cut and Bake:

    • Using a sharp knife, cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes before baking.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 40-50 minutes or until the baklava is golden brown.

  5. Prepare the Syrup:

    • While the baklava is baking, combine water, sugar, honey, cinnamon stick, and lemon juice in a saucepan.
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 15 minutes.
    • Remove the cinnamon stick and let the syrup cool.

  6. Pour the Syrup:

    • Once the baklava is out of the oven and still hot, slowly pour the cooled syrup over the hot baklava.

  7. Allow to Cool:

    • Allow the baklava to cool completely before serving. This will help the syrup to soak into the layers.

  8. Enjoy!

    • Once cooled, use a knife to cut along the previously made lines, and serve the delicious baklava.
