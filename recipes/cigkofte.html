Çiğköfte

Çiğ köfte or chee kofta is a kofta dish that is a regional specialty of southeastern Anatolia in Urfa. The dish is served as an appetizer or meze, and it is closely related with kibbeh nayyeh from Lebanese cuisine.

Traditionally made with raw meat, there are vegetarian variations made with bulgur and in Urfa, a local meatless version is made with scrambled eggs. In Diyarbakır province locally prepared batches are sold by street vendors.

Ingredients:

  • For the Çiğköfte:

    • 2 cups fine bulgur
    • 1 cup tomato paste
    • 1 cup fine-ground walnuts
    • 1 onion, finely grated
    • 1 tablespoon red pepper paste (optional, for extra heat)
    • 1 tablespoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
    • 1 tablespoon paprika
    • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
    • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
    • ½ cup olive oil
    • 1 cup hot water (approximately)

  • For Serving:

    • Lettuce leaves or flatbreads
    • Fresh lemon wedges
    • Fresh parsley, finely chopped
    • Radishes, thinly sliced (optional)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Bulgur:

    • Place the fine Bulgur in a large mixing bowl.
    • In a separate bowl, mix together the tomato paste, red pepper paste (if using), and hot water. Pour this mixture over the bulgur.

  2. Mix the Ingredients:

    • Add the grated onion, ground walnuts, salt, cumin, paprika, black pepper, cinnamon, allspice, cloves, coriander, and cayenne pepper to the bulgur mixture.

  3. Knead the Mixture:

    • Put on food-safe gloves and knead the mixture thoroughly for about 15-20 minutes until it becomes a cohesive and sticky dough. If the mixture is too dry, add a little more hot water.

  4. Add Olive Oil:

    • Add the olive oil to the mixture and knead for an additional 5 minutes. The oil helps to make the çiğköfte smooth and flavorful.

  5. Let it Rest:

    • Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let the çiğköfte mixture rest for at least 1-2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld.

  6. Shape and Serve:

    • Once rested, take a handful of the mixture and shape it into oval or round patties.
    • Serve the çiğköfte on lettuce leaves or flatbreads. Garnish with fresh lemon wedges, chopped parsley, and optional sliced radishes.

  7. Enjoy!

    • Squeeze some fresh lemon juice over the çiğköfte, wrap it in lettuce or flatbread, and enjoy this flavorful Turkish dish.

Note: Traditionally, çiğköfte is served with pomegranate molasses and extra lemon for those who prefer a tangy flavor. Adjust the spice level according to your taste preferences.
